# User Preferences

## Purpose

This file is the canonical preference override for homepage, About, philosophy, CTA, and other personal portfolio copy in this repo.

Use it before applying broader doctrine when the work is about Jayaram's own site.

Primary source notes:

- `../../portfolio_resources/guides-and-preferences/identity-and-profiles/homepage-copy-preferences.md`
- `../../portfolio_resources/guides-and-preferences/identity-and-profiles/about-me.md`

## Override rule

If generic doctrine and Jayaram's stated preferences conflict, follow the preferences for this repo.

## Name preference

- visible homepage or nav brand: `Jay`
- full-name contexts such as metadata or footer: `Jayaram` or `Jayaram H`

## Voice preference

- plain language over polished writing
- useful over impressive
- confident over fancy
- product-focused more than purely mechanical
- balanced builder plus thinker
- no cliche portfolio language
- no prestige wording
- no fake-deep contrast
- no slogan-first writing
- no AI-sounding filler
- no portfolio-template phrasing
- do not over-trim copy until it feels empty or undersold

## What the copy should signal

- he can build this well
- he can solve hard product problems
- he is a new product design mechanical engineer
- he learns fast, adapts, improves workflow, and uses tools that help him move faster

## Product philosophy preference

The page should imply:

- useful first
- functional first
- aesthetically good
- not overengineered
- simple enough to build

Do not turn this into manifesto copy.

## Homepage structure preference

Preferred flow at this stage:

1. hero
2. about
3. selected work
4. philosophy
5. contact

## Section-specific preferences

### Hero

- keep the main hero to two lines
- keep `Jay` in the hero
- `since 2019` can appear as a quiet supporting line
- avoid generic hero slogans
- avoid jargon-heavy technical phrasing in the main hero

### About

- sound like a real person speaking plainly
- do not undersell the work
- do not overemphasize packaging as the main identity
- do not frame the work as `small design decisions`
- emphasize product design, mechanical problem solving, and turning ideas into something buildable

### Experience

- avoid bullets for the current homepage experience section
- use short stacked lines instead
- make the copy fuller and more specific than generic role summaries
- avoid resume sludge
- avoid placeholder LinkedIn-summary writing

### Philosophy

- short supporting line on the right side is preferred
- supporting line should feel like the same family as `A few projects I've documented so far.`
- avoid cliche values language
- avoid fancy manifesto language
- keep it practical and product-focused
- short does not mean thin; a section can carry a little more substance if the copy still feels natural

### CTA

- `Email me` alone is too bland
- CTA should imply why someone would reach out
- tone should be confident and product-focused
- broad audience is acceptable: founders, product teams, and companies
- avoid clutter under the CTA

### Work grid

- keep case-study cards consistent with each other
- do not mix overlay and below-image card systems on the homepage
- supporting text families should feel visually consistent across sections

## Editing rule

If a line sounds too polished, too clever, too abstract, too consultant-like, or too generic, rewrite it in simpler and more direct language.

## Collaboration preference

- if a choice is needed, offer concrete options instead of asking broad open-ended questions
