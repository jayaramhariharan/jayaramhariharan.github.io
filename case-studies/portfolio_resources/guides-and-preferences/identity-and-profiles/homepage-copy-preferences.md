# Homepage Copy Preferences

## Purpose

This file captures Jayaram's active homepage and voice preferences for this repo.

Use it when writing or rewriting homepage, About, philosophy, CTA, and short portfolio copy.

## Current name preference

- use `Jay` in the visible homepage/nav brand
- use `Jayaram` or `Jayaram H` only where full-name context makes sense, such as metadata or footer

## Voice rules

- plain language over polished writing
- useful over impressive
- confident over fancy
- product-focused more than purely mechanical
- balanced builder + thinker
- no cliche portfolio language
- no prestige wording
- no fake-deep contrast like `screen vs real life`
- no slogan-first writing
- no AI-sounding filler
- no writerly framing that sounds like it was composed for a portfolio template
- do not over-trim copy until it feels empty or undersold

## What the copy should signal

- he can build this well
- he can solve hard product problems
- he is a new product design mechanical engineer
- he learns fast, adapts, and improves workflow
- he likes tools and methods that help him move faster
- he does not want to sound like a consultant, a pure mechanical engineer only, or a generic startup generalist

## Product philosophy direction

The page should imply:

- useful first
- functional first
- aesthetically good
- not overengineered
- simple enough to build

Do not turn this into manifesto copy.

## Homepage structure preferences

- keep the UI structure unless a better content order is clearly needed
- preferred homepage flow at this stage:
  - hero
  - about
  - selected work
  - philosophy
  - contact

## Hero preferences

- keep the main hero to two lines
- keep `Jay` in the hero, not `Jayaram`
- `since 2019` is acceptable as a quiet supporting line
- avoid generic hero slogans
- avoid jargon-heavy technical phrasing in the main hero

## About preferences

- sound like a real person speaking plainly
- do not undersell the work
- do not overemphasize packaging as the main identity
- do not frame the work as `small design decisions`
- stronger emphasis:
  - product design
  - mechanical problem solving
  - turning ideas into something buildable

## Experience section preferences

- avoid bullets for the current homepage experience section
- use short stacked lines instead
- make the copy fuller and more specific than generic role summaries
- avoid resume sludge
- avoid lines that feel like placeholder LinkedIn summaries

## Philosophy section preferences

- short supporting line on the right side is preferred
- supporting line should feel like the same family as:
  - `A few projects I've documented so far.`
- avoid Spartan fragments unless they still read naturally
- avoid cliche values language
- avoid fancy manifesto language
- philosophy should reflect practical product judgment, not abstract ideals
- short does not mean thin; a little more substance is better than empty copy

## CTA preferences

- `Email me` alone is too bland
- CTA should imply why someone would reach out
- tone should be confident and product-focused
- preferred audience is broad:
  - startups/founders
  - product teams/companies
- good CTA direction:
  - `Let's talk about the product`
- avoid clutter under the CTA

## Case-study grid preferences on homepage

- keep case-study cards consistent with each other
- avoid mixing overlay style on one card and below-image style on another
- supporting text families should feel visually consistent across sections

## Editing rule

If a line sounds:

- too polished
- too clever
- too abstract
- too consultant-like
- too generic

rewrite it in simpler, more direct language.

## Collaboration preference

- when input is needed, offer a few concrete options instead of broad open-ended questions
